    Strengths
    • First-mover advantage in B2B Ayurvedic niche with no direct competitors
    • Highly scalable SaaS model with recurring revenue (₹999-₹4999/month per practitioner)
    • Strong IP potential in the proprietary mapping algorithm between nutrition and Ayurveda
    • Low customer acquisition cost through Ayurvedic colleges and practitioner networks
    • Existing partnerships with 5 Ayurvedic colleges for pilot testing
    Weaknesses
    • Initial dependency on manually curated database of 500+ Indian foods with Ayurvedic properties
    • Requires validation by Ayurvedic experts (BAMS doctors) for each food property assignment
    • High initial development effort for core algorithm (estimated 900 developer hours)
    • Need for continuous updates to food database as new research emerges
    Opportunities
    • Growing global demand for wellness tech ($4.5T market) with increasing interest in Ayurveda
    • Potential for B2C mobile app spin-off targeting health-conscious consumers
    • Integration with diagnostic labs and health devices for automated data input
    • Government initiatives (AYUSH) promoting Ayurveda and digital health infrastructure
    • Export potential to countries with significant Ayurvedic practice (Germany, USA, UK)
    Threats
    • Slow adoption by traditionally-minded practitioners resistant to technology
    • Indirect competition from generic diet apps that may add Ayurvedic features
    • Regulatory changes in digital health space requiring compliance adjustments
    • Data privacy concerns requiring robust security measures
